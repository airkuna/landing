airKUNA02
The problem

Europe is building state identity and digital money —
but nothing connects them on an open network.

Every EU citizen is getting a government digital identity (EUDI Wallet) and regulated euro money is live under MiCA. Yet there is no open, composable layer that turns verified personhood into programmable euro. Two failures follow directly.

1

On-chain economies can't verify a real person

Without proof of personhood, one attacker spins up thousands of wallets and out-votes honest citizens. Sybil attacks break on-chain voting, quadratic funding, DAOs and any fair distribution of public money. Biometrics are privacy-hostile; social graphs collude; single-company KYC just creates a new gatekeeper.

2

Local value leaks out of the local economy

~90% of Croatian bank assets are foreign-owned; >€3bn of dividends left the country in 2022–24. Creators lose 15–40% to payment intermediaries. Money infrastructure is centralized, opaque, and extractive — the value created locally doesn't stay local.

The gap in one line: the trust anchor (state eID) and the money rail (regulated stablecoin) both exist — but the open, privacy-preserving bridge that makes them composable on a public chain does not. That bridge is airKUNA.

Sources: HNB / HUB, Bloomberg Adria, N1 (bank ownership & dividends); Patreon, Stripe (creator fees); ECB (money supply).

airKUNA04
The solution

Two composable primitives. One trustworthy local economy.

airKUNA ships the missing layer as two open, interoperable building blocks that plug into the eID and stablecoin infrastructure Europe is already mandating.

◇

Proof of Personhood — the moat

Verified eID login → a soulbound token (SBT) on-chain. One person = one identity across any number of wallets. Sybil-resistant, privacy-first (raw national ID never touches the chain), source-agnostic (Certilia today, EUDI tomorrow → all 27 EU states). This is the hard, defensible, regulation-aligned piece nobody else has.

€

Regulated euro stablecoin — the engine

A MiCA e-money token, backed 1:1 by euro, redeemable at par, settling in seconds on a public chain. Programmable money whose reserve yield funds the network — and whose value can stay in the local economy instead of leaking to foreign-owned finance.

Why the pairing wins: identity without money is a credential; money without identity is Sybil-attackable. Together they form the rails for personhood-gated voting, quadratic funding, participatory budgets, creator payments and local circular economies — composable by any DAO, municipality or cooperative, unlike the EU's permissioned EBSI.

Sybil-resistant

A deterministic, irreversible nullifier means 100 wallets of one person → one identity → one vote.

GDPR by design

Raw national ID never leaves the backend or hits the chain. Only an irreversible nullifier goes on-chain.

Source-agnostic

Certilia today → EUDI Wallet (SD-JWT VC / mDoc) tomorrow. Same on-chain layer, all 27 EU states.

eID = recovery

Lose a wallet? Re-login with eID → same nullifier → migrate identity to a new wallet.

Honest on trust: the MVP verifier (zkTLS over the eID session, or NFC chip) needs no signer network — the same proven approach as World ID, but rooted in state eID instead of biometrics. A decentralized signer mesh is a later phase that improves trust-minimization, not a precondition to ship.
